Lease Agreement:

  1. Clearly outline the duration of the lease (start and end dates).
  2. Specify whether it's a fixed-term lease or a month-to-month agreement.
    • If fixed-term, mention the exact duration (e.g., 12 months).
    • If month-to-month, explain the terms for termination with notice.

Rent Payment:

  1. State the monthly rent amount and the due date.
  2. Outline acceptable payment methods (e.g., bank transfer, check, etc.).
    • Provide details on where payments should be sent or made.
  3. Include any late fees for overdue payments.
    • Specify the amount of late fees and the grace period, if any.

Repairs and Maintenance:

  1. Clearly define the landlord's responsibilities for major repairs.
    • Specify what constitutes major repairs (e.g., structural issues).
    • Include a timeframe for the landlord to address major repairs.
  2. Specify the tenant's responsibilities for minor maintenance tasks.
    • Provide examples of minor tasks (e.g., changing light bulbs, yard maintenance).
  3. Establish a process for reporting and addressing maintenance issues.
    • Provide contact information for reporting issues.
    • Define the timeline for addressing reported maintenance problems.

Insurance:

  1. Specify whether the tenant is required to have renter's insurance.
    • Clarify the minimum coverage requirements for renter's insurance.
  2. Clarify the landlord's insurance coverage for the property.
    • Explain what the landlord's insurance covers and does not cover.

Legal Responsibilities:

  1. Clarify legal responsibilities of both parties.
    • Include information on property use in compliance with local laws.
  2. Include any applicable local laws and regulations.
    • Provide references to relevant local statutes or regulations.
